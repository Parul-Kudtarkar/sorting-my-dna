Parul Kudtarkar

Senior Bioinformatician using AI and machine learning to advance human health.

With over 20 years of experience spanning biomedical engineering, research, and data science, I lead two major initiatives at UC San Diego: PanKbase, a $12.5M NIH-funded project to build the first integrated pancreas knowledgebase, and the Accelerating Medicines Partnership in Common Metabolic Diseases, a global effort to transform how we understand and treat metabolic disorders.

With an undergraduate degree in biomedical engineering and a graduate degree in bioinformatics, I bring a systems-level perspective, from circuit boards and hospital instrumentation early in my career to developing AI and machine learning tools for single-cell genomics today. My professional journey through Harvard, Caltech, and UCSD reflects a lifelong mission to harness technology and data to improve human life.

I regularly engage with stakeholders, patients, and professionals from diverse backgrounds through conferences, collaborative meetings, and real-world conversations, bringing empathy, context, and lived experience into my scientific work. I believe this human connection is essential in translating research into real impact.

Outside work, I'm a runner, traveler, painter, wildlife advocate, and passionate home cook.

Artworks

As an aspiring painter, I draw inspiration from art guide books, incorporating their images and techniques into my artwork. This approach enables me to give life to my own distinct interpretation and style, despite being a novice in the field of painting.
